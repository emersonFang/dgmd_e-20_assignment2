Learning to play an instrument should be fun and relaxing, but takes a good deal of concentration. I recommend practicing for 30 minutes to an hour at a time, and then taking a 15 minute break if you want to do a marathon run of practicing for several hours in the day. It's probably unhealthy to practice 8 hours straight through.

Slow practice is great for getting your brain to comprehend what needs to be done physically to produce the best sound. Even though it might be boring, it helps train you focus and strategize for the best physical execution of how to perform something. Once you have played something 10 times in a row perfectly at a certain speed, increase the tempo by turning up your metronome.

Rhythms are also great for learning how to play fast runs on an instrument. Dotted rhytms, such as sixteenth note plus a dotted eighth, or vice versa, are a good starting point. Next you can go on to playing triplets to increase the speed of the run. Repeating this method is a good way to train yourself to do something well. It can be a fun exercise.

Always start playing scales slowly and listen to the relationship between the notes slowly and carefully. That way you can train your ear to hear the interval between the notes better and improve your intonation. On the violin, certain notes might cause the violin to resonate more if they are played in tune, such as a note that corresponds to an open string, or any of the fifths or thirds correspond to the open strings.
